Brian Lau

"The master has failed more times than the apprentice has tried"

I am Brian Lau, a developer living in Portland, Oregon. My family is from China and Hong Kong. I have an MBA and a BA from the University of Portland and the University of Washington, respectively. I like to play basketball, bring people together, and create things.


1988

My story with computers began in 1995 when our family got our first IBM Windows 95 computer. It costed over $2000 and I vaguely remember deleting all the system files in an attempt to create more space. Since then, I've always been the go-to computer guy to fix things. Sometimes our failures have the adverse effect on us.


2006

I began college in Seattle at the University of Washington. Going to college there changed my perspective completely. I found an incredible group of friends, I was exposed to great ideas, and I struggled. Hard. I realized I coasted through high school and now, I was in a league with very bright people.


2010

I graduated and learned that my dad had lung cancer and about a year left to live. This was the true learning experience. I got my first job doing office work, was teaching Sunday School, taking a course at Portland State, and studying for the GMAT. I passed.


2012

Needing a change, I enrolled in a 6-month Chinese Language Program at Tsinghua University in Beijing, China - the one that Mark Zuckerberg speaks at. I felt very comfortable there as I was no longer a minority. I was also living the dream, I had always wanted to learn Chinese, and each day it felt like what I learned in class, I could later use in the day. Spring 2012 at Tsinghua was a very special time and a needed getaway. When I returned, I began my MBA program.


2015

After a short stint at Nike, I completed my MBA, and realized I wanted to code. I enrolled at Epicodus and did that. Along with meeting some really interesting people, I am really happy that I can now develop websites and applications. Let's see where the road goes next.
